Design Challenge

How to overcome Meg's House design issues

What is the problem you are trying to solve?

The problem I am trying to solve is helping women and children struggling with domestic violence.

Take a stab at framing it as a design question.

How can Meg's House redesign ensure women and children feel safe and empowered during their struggles?

Now state the ultimate impact you are trying to have.

The ultimate impact I am trying to have on these women is to make sure they feel included and know that they can trust this company and its values.

What are some possible solutions to your problem? Think broadly. It's fine to start a project with a hunch or two, but make sure you allow for surprising outcomes.

Some possible solutions that can be done to help Meg's House is developing a new logo, including a color change. The current color choice has a hint of red which is one of the most anxiety inducing colors; this alone is a reason people may not want to visit the site. Another solution is looking into different typeface, maybe considering san-serif becuase it is a more modern type and this is a modern/everyday issue.

Finally, write down some of the context and constraints that you're facing. They could be geographic, technological, time-based, or have to do with the population you are trying to reach.

One constraint that may be an issue, is this location being outside of Anderson, so I may not be able to visit the acutal location. Also, finding a user that struggles with this issue to get some good insight from may be an issue, as not everyone is comfortable discussing their expereinces.

Does your original question need a tweak? Try it again.

How can Meg's House clearly show that they are a trusted non-proft organization and ensure they can make these women feel empowered while knowing they can trust the company and their values?
